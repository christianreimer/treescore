Treescore

Score a Christmas Tree based on uniformity of lights, shape of tree, and colors used.

View the Project on GitHub christianreimer/treescore

Computer Vision to Amuse and Delight

Obsessing over the distribution of your Christman Twingle Lights? Wondering if your tree is standing straight, or maybe leaning just a little bit to wards the wall? Too many red ribbons? Not enough glitter? Too many ornaments over here and a big gap over there?

Light Uniformity Score

To detemine the uniformity of the lights on the tree, I first locate all the lights by identifying the brightest regions of the image, and assume the center of each region correspond to a light. Then, for each light, I take the average distance between that light and its three closest neighbors. Finally, I take the standard deviation of all these averages. In addition, I want to reward trees that have more lights (while still maintaining uniformity), so the score is scaled up by the number of lights.

The score returned is calculated as 

light_score = 100 - log(num_lights) * std_dev(avg_light_dist)

Tree Shape Score

The shape of the tree is compared to a triangle with the ratio of XX/YY. Where did that come from? I measured a picture of a trees that I found to be visually appealing ... Yes, completely subjective. In addition to judging the height/width ratio, I compare the observed angle of the tree (from the middle point between bottom left and bottom right corners to the top) with a vertical line and calculate the angle in degrees.

The score returned is calculated as 

shape_score = 100 - abs(ideal_ratio - observed_ratio) * angle

Color Score

The ideal Christmas colors are green, red, gold, and white. I somewhat arbitrarely decidede that a pleasent distribution of colors was 50% green, 20% red, 20% gold, and 10% white. So that is what I am scoring on.

The score returned is calculated as 

color_score = 100 - sum(abs(ideal_ratio[c] - observed_ratio[c])
                        for c in ['green', 'red', 'gold', white'])

Combined Score

Finally the scores are combined 

total_score = sum(light_score, shape_score, color_score) / 3
