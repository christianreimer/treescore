Treescore

Score a Christmas Tree based on uniformity of lights, shape of tree, and colors used.

Computer Vision to Amuse and Delight

Obsessing over the distribution of your Christman Twingle Lights? Wondering if your tree is standing straight, or maybe leaning just a little bit to wards the wall? Too many red ribbons? Not enough glitter? Too many ornaments over here and a big gap over there?

Light Uniformity Score

To detemine the uniformity of the lights on the tree, we first locate all the lights by identifying the brightest regions of the image. Then, for each light, I take the average distance between that light and its 3 closest neighbors. Finally, I take the standard deviation of all these averages. In addition, I want to reward trees that have more lights (while still maintaining uniformity), so the score is scaled up by the number of lights. The speficic score returned is calculated as score = 100 - ((3 / log(number_of_lights) * std_dev(average distance between beighboring lights)
